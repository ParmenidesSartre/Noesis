# Feature 8: Learning Materials & Resources

**Document Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2026-01-26

---

## 8.1 Study Material Repository

### 8.1.1 Material Organization

**Repository Structure:**
- Hierarchical organization:
  - By Subject (Math, Science, English, etc.)
    - By Grade Level (Primary 1-6, Secondary 1-5, etc.)
      - By Topic/Chapter
        - By Material Type (Notes, Worksheets, Practice, etc.)

**Material Categories:**
- Study notes/summaries
- Lecture slides
- Worksheets
- Practice questions
- Past year papers
- Sample answers/solutions
- Reference materials
- Cheat sheets/formula sheets
- Mind maps/concept maps
- Infographics

**Metadata and Tagging:**
- Title
- Description
- Subject and grade level
- Topic/chapter tags
- Material type
- Difficulty level (Easy/Medium/Hard)
- Language
- File type (PDF, PPT, Word, etc.)
- File size
- Uploaded by (teacher name)
- Upload date
- Last updated
- Version number (if updates made)

### 8.1.2 Uploading Materials

**Upload Process:**
- Teachers and admins can upload materials
- Drag-and-drop file upload
- Bulk upload (multiple files at once)
- Upload from cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive integration)
- File format support:
  - Documents (PDF, Word, PowerPoint, Excel)
  - Images (JPG, PNG, GIF)
  - Audio (MP3)
  - Video (MP4, link to external video)
  - Compressed (ZIP, RAR)
- Maximum file size limits (e.g., 100MB per file, configurable)

**Material Details Entry:**
- Fill in metadata (subject, topic, description, tags)
- Set access permissions:
  - All students
  - Specific grade levels
  - Specific classes
  - Specific students
  - Private (teacher only)
  - Public (visible on website)
- Copyright and attribution (if material from external source)

**Version Control:**
- Update existing material with new version
- Keep version history
- Students can access previous versions if needed
- Change log (what was updated)

### 8.1.3 Accessing and Downloading Materials

**Student/Parent Access:**
- Browse material repository
- Filter by:
  - Subject
  - Grade level
  - Topic
  - Material type
  - Difficulty
- Search by keywords
- Recently added materials
- Most downloaded materials
- Recommended materials (based on student's classes)

**Viewing and Downloading:**
- Preview material (PDF viewer, image viewer)
- Download material to device
- Print material (if allowed)
- Share material (if allowed, e.g., share link with classmates)

**Access Permissions:**
- Materials restricted to enrolled students (per subject/class)
- Some materials public (for marketing, free resources)
- Download limits (if applicable, e.g., max 10 downloads per day)
- Track who downloaded what (analytics)

**Favorites and Bookmarks:**
- Students can save favorite materials
- Create personal folders/collections
- Quick access to frequently used materials

### 8.1.4 Material Management

**Admin Controls:**
- Approve/reject uploaded materials (if approval workflow enabled)
- Edit material details
- Delete materials
- Move materials between categories
- Feature materials on homepage

**Quality Control:**
- Review materials for accuracy and appropriateness
- Flag outdated materials
- Request updates from teachers
- Standardize file naming conventions

**Usage Analytics:**
- Track material downloads
- Most popular materials
- Underutilized materials
- Usage by student, class, grade level

**Material Cleanup:**
- Archive old materials
- Delete unused materials
- Periodic review and refresh

## 8.2 Video Library

### 8.2.1 Video Content Management

**Video Types:**
- Recorded lessons (full class recordings)
- Lecture videos (teacher explanations)
- Tutorial videos (step-by-step guides)
- Demonstration videos (experiments, practical work)
- Revision videos (exam tips, summaries)
- Student presentations
- Guest lectures
- Educational series

**Video Hosting:**
- Host videos on platform (requires video storage)
- OR embed videos from external platforms:
  - YouTube
  - Vimeo
  - Google Drive
  - OneDrive
  - Custom video server
- Hybrid approach (mix of hosted and embedded)

**Video Upload:**
- Teachers upload video files
- OR paste video URL (for YouTube, etc.)
- Video details:
  - Title
  - Description
  - Subject and grade
  - Topic
  - Duration
  - Thumbnail image
  - Tags
  - Transcript (optional, for accessibility and SEO)
- Processing: Video transcoding (for different quality levels)

**Video Organization:**
- Organized by subject, grade, topic (same as study materials)
- Video playlists/series
- Curated collections (e.g., "Exam Preparation Series")

### 8.2.2 Video Player Features

**Player Interface:**
- Play/pause, volume control
- Playback speed control (0.5x, 1x, 1.25x, 1.5x, 2x)
- Full-screen mode
- Picture-in-picture mode
- Quality selection (if multiple qualities available)
- Subtitles/captions (if available)
- Skip forward/backward (10 sec)

**Interactive Features:**
- In-video quizzes (pause video, ask question, resume after answering)
- Bookmarks/timestamps (student marks important parts)
- Notes (students take notes while watching, linked to video timestamp)
- Chapter markers (jump to specific sections)

**Watch Progress:**
- Track how much of video student watched
- Resume from last position
- Mark video as watched/completed
- Watch time analytics

**Access Control:**
- Restrict video access to enrolled students
- Free preview (first 2 minutes) for non-enrolled
- Download video (if allowed, or stream only)
- Prevent unauthorized sharing (watermark, DRM)

### 8.2.3 Live Streaming

**Live Class Streaming:**
- Stream live classes to absent students or remote students
- Integration with video conferencing tools:
  - Zoom
  - Google Meet
  - Microsoft Teams
  - YouTube Live
  - Custom streaming server
- Schedule live streams
- Send notifications before live stream starts

**Live Interaction:**
- Live chat during stream
- Q&A (students ask questions during live session)
- Polls (teacher launches polls to check understanding)
- Raise hand (virtual hand raise for questions)

**Live Recording:**
- Auto-record live streams
- Save to video library after stream ends
- Edit recording before publishing (trim, add intro/outro)

**Playback:**
- Replay live stream as on-demand video
- Access recording immediately or after processing

**Collaborative Documents:**
- Google Docs integration (collaborative editing)

- Version history
- Comment and suggestions mode

### 8.3.3 Document Viewer

- View documents without downloading
- PDF viewer, Word viewer, etc.
- Full-screen reading mode
- Zoom in/out
- Page navigation
- Print document (if allowed)

**Annotations:**
- Highlight text
- Add comments/notes
- Draw on document (for PDFs)
- Save annotations (personal, not visible to others)

**Document Download:**
- Download original file
- Download as PDF (if converted)
- Download with annotations (if supported)

## 8.4 Assignment Worksheets

### 8.4.1 Worksheet Creation

**Worksheet Builder:**
- Template-based worksheet creation
- Drag-and-drop elements:
  - Text blocks
  - Questions (multiple choice, fill-in-blank, short answer)
  - Images
  - Tables
  - Instructions
- Worksheet sections (Part A, Part B, etc.)
- Answer key (separate document or included with teacher copy)

**Worksheet Types:**
- Practice worksheets
- Homework worksheets
- In-class activities
- Assessments
- Revision worksheets
- Diagnostic worksheets

**Worksheet Formatting:**
- Customizable layout
- Header/footer (student name, class, date fields)
- Branding (centre logo)
- Page numbering
- Print-friendly format

### 8.4.2 Distributing Worksheets

**Assignment:**
- Assign worksheet to class or students
- Set due date
- Link to assignment (students submit completed worksheet)
- Or just for practice (no submission required)

**Distribution Methods:**
- Downloadable (students print and complete)
- Online fillable (students complete on device)
- Printable from parent portal
- Email to students/parents
- Provided physically in class

**Answer Key Distribution:**
- Release answer key after deadline
- OR immediately (for self-study)
- OR never (teacher reviews in class)

### 8.4.3 Worksheet Management

**Worksheet Library:**
- Store all worksheets by subject, topic
- Reuse worksheets term after term
- Share worksheets among teachers
- Rate and review worksheets (teacher collaboration)

**Worksheet Analytics:**
- Track usage (how many students downloaded)
- Track completion (if linked to assignment)
- Average time spent (if online fillable)
- Identify difficult questions (from submitted answers)

## 8.5 Practice Tests

### 8.5.1 Practice Test Creation

**Test Builder:**
- Same as quiz builder (see Section 6.2)
- Specifically marked as "Practice" (not graded formally)
- Question bank for common test types (e.g., PSLE practice, SPM practice)

**Practice Test Types:**
- Topic-based practice (e.g., Algebra practice test)
- Full-length mock exams
- Timed practice (simulate real exam conditions)
- Adaptive practice (difficulty adjusts based on performance)

**Test Settings:**
- Unlimited attempts (for practice)
- Immediate feedback (show correct answers after submission)
- Hints available (if student stuck)
- Explain wrong answers (rationale for correct answer)

### 8.5.2 Taking Practice Tests

**Student Experience:**
- Browse available practice tests
- Filter by subject, topic, difficulty
- Start practice test
- Timer (if timed practice)
- Submit and view results immediately
- Review answers
- See explanations
- Retake test

**Performance Tracking:**
- Track scores over multiple attempts
- Improvement trend
- Identify weak topics (based on wrong answers)
- Recommended practice (system suggests tests based on weak areas)

**Gamification:**
- Earn points/badges for completing practice tests
- Leaderboard (optional, if competitive environment desired)
- Streak tracking (consecutive days practicing)

### 8.5.3 Practice Test Analytics

**Individual Analytics:**
- Student's practice test history
- Average score
- Improvement over time
- Time spent practicing
- Topics mastered vs need more practice

**Class Analytics:**
- Class average on practice tests
- Common mistakes (which questions students get wrong)
- Topics needing more review (for teacher planning)

**Test Quality:**
- Feedback on test difficulty
- Question effectiveness (are questions too easy/hard?)
- Adjust question bank based on data

## 8.6 Digital Library

### 8.6.1 Library Catalog

**Digital Book Collection:**
- eBooks (ePub, PDF, Mobi)
- Audiobooks
- Reference books
- Textbooks (digital versions)
- Supplementary readers
- Magazines and journals
- Comics and graphic novels (educational)

**Cataloging:**
- Book details:
  - Title, author, publisher
  - ISBN, edition
  - Subject category
  - Grade/age level
  - Language
  - Number of pages
  - Publication year
  - Synopsis/description
  - Cover image
  - Recommended reading level (lexile score, etc.)
- Metadata tags (genre, theme, topic)

**Library Organization:**
- Browse by subject
- Browse by grade level
- Browse by genre
- Curated collections (e.g., "Summer Reading List", "STEM Books")
- New arrivals
- Popular/Trending
- Staff picks

### 8.6.2 Book Borrowing and Access

**Digital Borrowing:**
- Students can "borrow" eBooks
- Borrowing limits (e.g., 3 books at a time)
- Loan period (e.g., 14 days)
- Auto-return after loan period (for DRM-protected eBooks)
- Renewals (if no waitlist)
- Holds/Reservations (if book currently borrowed by someone else)

**Reading Interface:**
- eBook reader (in-browser or app)
- Adjustable font size, style
- Day/night mode
- Highlighting and notes
- Bookmarks
- Dictionary lookup (tap word to see definition)
- Text-to-speech (audiobook feature)

**Access Control:**
- Library access for enrolled students
- Some free books for public (marketing)
- Premium books (require subscription or purchase)

**Download Options:**
- Read online (streaming)
- Download for offline reading (if allowed)
- DRM protection (prevent unauthorized sharing)

### 8.6.3 Library Management

**Adding Books:**
- Admin uploads eBooks
- Purchase eBook licenses
- Partner with eBook platforms (Epic!, OverDrive, etc.)
- Free public domain books (Project Gutenberg)

**Circulation Management:**
- Track borrowed books
- Overdue notices
- Waitlist management
- Most borrowed books
- Recommend books to students (based on reading history)

**Reading Analytics:**
- Books borrowed per student
- Reading completion rate
- Time spent reading
- Popular books
- Reading level progress
- Reading challenges (e.g., "Read 10 books this term")

**Library Reports:**
- Circulation reports
- Collection usage
- Reading trends
- Acquisition recommendations (which books to add)
